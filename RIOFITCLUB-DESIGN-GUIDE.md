# Rio Fit Club Brand Guide

## Brand Position

Rio Fit Club is a kinetic fitness, language, and social club for people who want Rio to feel less like a destination and more like a life they can enter.

The brand should feel premium, social, athletic, modern, and unmistakably Rio. It should not look like a budget gym chain, a corporate wellness app, or a generic bodybuilding logo.

## Core Idea

Train where the city moves.

The visual identity is built around motion: coast, climb, track, rhythm, sweat, language, and community. The logo is called the Motion Crest.

### Logo Rules

- Use the horizontal logo for web headers, signage, ads, and documents.
- Use the mark for app icons, social avatars, apparel, stickers, and event badges.
- Use the stacked lockup for posters, square crops, event backdrops, and merch.
- Use the one-color logo for embroidery, engraving, stamps, and budget production.
- Minimum digital mark size: 32 px.
- Minimum horizontal logo width: 180 px.
- Keep at least one motion-stroke width of clearspace around the mark.
- Do not stretch, recolor randomly, add generic shadows, or pair with dumbbells, flames, shields, or flexed arms.

## Color System

The system is dark first, electric second, with Rio color in controlled flashes.

| Token | Hex | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Night Court | `#07100A` | Main background, premium depth |
| Deep Green | `#0C150F` | Secondary background, sections |
| Surface Green | `#141E17` | Cards, panels, nav surfaces |
| Warm Chalk | `#F6FFF5` | Headlines, high-contrast text |
| Muted Sage | `#B9CBBC` | Body copy, secondary text |
| Pulse Green | `#19F5A0` | CTAs, progress, active states, logo recognition |
| Green Hot | `#53FFAB` | Hover states and high-energy campaign moments |
| Pool Cyan | `#18D4E7` | Language, water, welcome, travel, discovery |
| Sunset Coral | `#FF6A4D` | Social events, nightlife, limited drops, campaign sparks |

### Color Ratio

- 70% dark surfaces.
- 20% chalk and muted text.
- 7% pulse green.
- 3% cyan and coral.

The green should remain the conversion color. Cyan and coral should prevent the brand from becoming one-note.

## Typography

### Display: Anton

Use Anton for hero headlines, event posters, big commands, large numbers, and campaign statements. It gives the brand athletic punch and is stronger than the current Lexend-only guide.

Rules:

- Use uppercase for major brand statements.
- Keep letter spacing at `0` or slightly positive.
- Do not use negative tracking.
- Keep line-height tight: `0.86` to `0.98` for large headlines.

### Body: Manrope

Use Manrope for body copy, forms, buttons, labels, captions, course materials, and UI text.

Rules:

- Body size: 16-18 px.
- Body line-height: 1.5-1.65.
- Labels: 12-14 px, 800-900 weight, uppercase, positive letter spacing.
- Buttons: 800-900 weight, uppercase, no text wrapping on desktop.

## Graphic System

The three motion strokes from the logo become the visual language.

### Coast Line

Color: Pool Cyan.  
Use for language, welcome, travel, water, discovery, and navigation.

### Training Line

Color: Pulse Green.  
Use for CTAs, progress, class completion, performance, and active states.

### Social Line

Color: Sunset Coral.  
Use for gatherings, nightlife, post-workout moments, launch campaigns, and limited edition merch.

## UI Principles

- Dark backgrounds should carry the system.
- Use stable 8 px radii for cards, panels, buttons, and form fields.
- Use one primary green CTA per viewport.
- Add active button feedback with a subtle `scale(.97)`.
- Use ease-out transitions around 140-220 ms.
- Avoid large decorative color blobs or generic gradient backgrounds.
- Use real Rio, training, community, and discipline imagery whenever possible.
- Do not put cards inside cards.
- Keep section layouts full-width and composed, not overly decorative.

## Photography Direction

Images should show actual movement, people, sweat, Rio light, and mixed community.

Use:

- Beach training at sunrise or late afternoon.
- Combat sports, lifting, cycling, surf, run club, volleyball, tennis, yoga.
- Locals and expats together.
- Real social moments after training.
- High-contrast gym lighting with green or cyan accent.

Avoid:

- Generic stock fitness poses.
- Dark blurred bodies where the activity is unclear.
- Corporate wellness smiles.
- Overly luxury pool-and-champagne imagery.
- One body type or one version of Rio.

## Voice

Rio Fit Club copy should be direct, social, and alive.

Say:

- Train where the city moves.
- Come for the workout. Stay for the people.
- Rio access starts here.
- Language in motion.
- Stronger in rhythm.

Avoid:

- Generic motivation.
- Empty luxury claims.
- Overpromising transformation.
- Sterile wellness language.
- Aggressive alpha-fitness language.

## Application Rules

### Signage

Use the horizontal logo in Warm Chalk and Pulse Green on Night Court. Add cyan or coral edge stripes only when the environment supports color.

### Apparel

Use the mark large on backs, small on chest, and one-color for caps. Use coral for limited drops only.

### Social

Use Anton headlines, a real action image, one motion stroke, and one CTA. Avoid using every brand color in one post.

### Events

Use cyan for language/welcome events, green for training events, and coral for social/nightlife events.
